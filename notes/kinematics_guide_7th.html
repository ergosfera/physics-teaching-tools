01

Definition of a Variable

Theory

In physics, a variable is a symbol that represents a quantity that can take different values. Variables let us write general rules instead of repeating the same calculation for every possible number.

Each variable has a name, a symbol, and a unit. For example:

Name Symbol Unit
positionxmeter (m)
timetsecond (s)
speed / velocityvmeter per second (m/s)
accelerationameter per second squared (m/s²)

A variable is not the same as a number. The symbol t can equal 2 s in one problem and 15 s in another — the symbol stays the same, the value changes.

Worked Example

A car travels for some time t and covers a distance d. Identify the variables, their symbols, and their units.

Given info: two physical quantities — time and distance.
Symbol for time: t  ·  unit: second (s)
Symbol for distance: d (or Δx)  ·  unit: meter (m)

→ t [s] and d [m] are the two variables in this situation.

Practice

  1. List three physical quantities you can measure about a moving bicycle. For each one, write its name, a symbol you would use, and its unit.
  2. A swimmer completes a race. Which variable tells you how far she swam? Which variable tells you how long it took?
SHOW ANSWERS

1. Possible answers: distance — d or Δx — m; time — t — s; speed — v — m/s. (Others are valid.)

2. Distance → Δx [m]; time → t [s].

02

What Does Δ (Delta) Mean?

Theory

The Greek letter Δ (delta) means "change in." It is always calculated as:

Δ(quantity) = final value − initial value

So Δx means "change in position" and Δt means "change in time." The initial value is sometimes written with a subscript i or the number 0, and the final value with a subscript f:

Δx = x_f − x_i Δt = t_f − t_i

Delta can be positive (quantity increased), negative (quantity decreased), or zero (no change).

Worked Example

A runner starts at position x = 5 m and finishes at x = 23 m. The clock reads 10 s at the start and 18 s at the finish. Find Δx and Δt.

Δx: x_f − x_i = 23 m − 5 m = 18 m
Δt: t_f − t_i = 18 s − 10 s = 8 s

→ Δx = 18 m  |  Δt = 8 s

Practice

  1. A car's speedometer reads 12 m/s at t = 3 s and 30 m/s at t = 9 s. Calculate Δv and Δt.
  2. A ball is at position x = 40 m. It moves and ends up at x = 15 m. What is Δx? Is it positive or negative? What does that tell you about the direction of motion?
SHOW ANSWERS

1. Δv = 30 − 12 = 18 m/s; Δt = 9 − 3 = 6 s.

2. Δx = 15 − 40 = −25 m. Negative → the ball moved in the negative direction (backward / to the left).

03

Definition of Speed

Theory

Speed tells you how much distance an object covers per unit of time. It answers the question: how fast?

v = Δx / Δt

Speed is always a positive number (or zero). Its unit is m/s (meters per second). Speed does not tell you which direction the object is moving.

When the speed stays the same throughout a trip, we call it constant speed. When it changes, we often talk about average speed, which uses the total distance and total time.

Worked Example

A cyclist covers 150 m in 25 s. What is her speed?

Identify: Δx = 150 m, Δt = 25 s, v = ?
Formula: v = Δx / Δt
Substitute: v = 150 m / 25 s = 6 m/s

→ v = 6 m/s

Practice

  1. A dog runs 48 m in 8 s. What is its speed?
  2. A train travels at 25 m/s. How far does it go in 2 minutes? (Careful with units!)
SHOW ANSWERS

1. v = 48 / 8 = 6 m/s.

2. Δt = 2 min = 120 s; Δx = v · Δt = 25 × 120 = 3000 m.

04

Speed vs. Velocity

Theory

Velocity is speed with direction. It uses the same formula, but Δx is a signed displacement (positive or negative depending on direction), not just a distance:

v = Δx / Δt   (with sign)

Key contrasts:

Property Speed Velocity
Has direction?NoYes
Can be negative?NoYes
Type of quantityscalarvector
Example12 m/s−12 m/s (moving left)

A car going 60 km/h north and a car going 60 km/h south have the same speed but opposite velocities.

Worked Example

A ball rolls from x = 20 m to x = 8 m in 4 s. Find (a) the speed and (b) the velocity.

Δx: 8 − 20 = −12 m   (displacement, signed)
Distance: |−12| = 12 m   (always positive)
(a) Speed: 12 m / 4 s = 3 m/s
(b) Velocity: −12 m / 4 s = −3 m/s (moving in the negative direction)

→ speed = 3 m/s  |  velocity = −3 m/s

Practice

  1. A skater moves from x = 0 m to x = −30 m in 6 s. Find her speed and her velocity.
  2. Two objects both have a speed of 5 m/s. Object A moves to the right; object B moves to the left. Write their velocities using signs. Are they the same?
SHOW ANSWERS

1. Δx = −30 m; speed = 30/6 = 5 m/s; velocity = −30/6 = −5 m/s.

2. v_A = +5 m/s; v_B = −5 m/s. Same speed, different velocities.

05

Uniform Motion — Word Problems

Theory

Uniform motion (also called constant-velocity motion) means the object moves at a fixed speed in a fixed direction — no speeding up, no slowing down, no turning. Acceleration equals zero.

There is only one equation you need:

Δx = v · Δt

This can be rearranged to find any of the three variables:

v = Δx / Δt Δt = Δx / v

Problem-solving steps: (1) identify what you know, (2) identify what you want, (3) choose the right form of the equation, (4) substitute and calculate, (5) check the unit.

Worked Example

A boat moves at a constant velocity of 4 m/s. How long does it take to cross a river that is 60 m wide?

Known: v = 4 m/s, Δx = 60 m
Want: Δt
Equation: Δt = Δx / v
Substitute: Δt = 60 m / 4 m/s = 15 s

→ Δt = 15 s

Practice

  1. A bus moves at 12 m/s for 30 s. How far does it travel?
  2. A person walks 270 m at a constant speed of 1.5 m/s. How long does the walk take?
SHOW ANSWERS

1. Δx = 12 × 30 = 360 m.

2. Δt = 270 / 1.5 = 180 s.

06

Uniform Motion — Graphs

Theory

Two graphs are used to represent uniform motion:

Position-time graph (x vs. t): produces a straight diagonal line. The slope of that line equals the velocity: slope = Δx / Δt = v.

Velocity-time graph (v vs. t): produces a horizontal straight line because velocity does not change.

The area under a v-t graph equals the displacement Δx.

Worked Example

An x-t graph shows a straight line passing through (0 s, 2 m) and (5 s, 22 m). What is the velocity of the object?

Slope = Δx / Δt: (22 − 2) m / (5 − 0) s
Calculate: 20 m / 5 s = 4 m/s

→ v = 4 m/s (positive → moving in positive direction)

Practice

  1. An x-t graph has a straight line through (0 s, 0 m) and (8 s, 24 m). What is the object's velocity?
  2. A v-t graph shows a horizontal line at v = 5 m/s from t = 0 to t = 10 s. What is the displacement during this interval?
SHOW ANSWERS

1. slope = 24/8 = 3 m/s.

2. Δx = area = 5 × 10 = 50 m.

07

Definition of Acceleration

Theory

Acceleration measures how quickly velocity changes. It answers: how fast is the speed changing?

a = Δv / Δt

Its unit is m/s² (meters per second squared). Like velocity, acceleration has a sign: positive means speeding up in the positive direction (or slowing down in the negative direction); negative means the opposite.

Important: an object can have acceleration even when it is momentarily at rest — it just means its velocity is about to change.

Worked Example

A motorcycle increases its velocity from 6 m/s to 24 m/s in 6 s. What is its acceleration?

Δv: 24 − 6 = 18 m/s
Δt: 6 s
a = Δv / Δt: 18 / 6 = 3 m/s²

→ a = 3 m/s² (speeding up)

Practice

  1. A car slows from 20 m/s to 8 m/s in 4 s. Find the acceleration. Is it positive or negative?
  2. An object has a = 0. What can you say about its motion?
SHOW ANSWERS

1. Δv = 8 − 20 = −12 m/s; a = −12 / 4 = −3 m/s². Negative → slowing down.

2. a = 0 means Δv = 0, so velocity is constant → uniform motion (or the object is at rest).

08

Uniformly Accelerated Motion — Word Problems

Theory

Uniformly Accelerated Motion (UAM) means the acceleration is constant — the velocity changes by the same amount every second. The three kinematic equations for UAM are:

v_f = v_i + a · Δt Δx = v_i · Δt + ½ · a · Δt² v_f² = v_i² + 2 · a · Δx

Each equation uses four of the five kinematic variables: v_i, v_f, a, Δt, Δx. The strategy is to identify three known quantities and choose the equation that contains those three plus the one unknown.

Worked Example

A ball starts from rest and accelerates at 2 m/s² for 5 s. How far does it travel?

Known: v_i = 0 m/s, a = 2 m/s², Δt = 5 s
Want: Δx
Equation: Δx = v_i · Δt + ½ · a · Δt²
Substitute: Δx = 0·5 + ½·2·5² = 0 + 1·25 = 25 m

→ Δx = 25 m

Practice

  1. A skier starts at 3 m/s and accelerates at 1.5 m/s² for 8 s. What is her final velocity?
  2. A car traveling at 20 m/s brakes with a = −4 m/s². How far does it travel before stopping?
SHOW ANSWERS

1. v_f = 3 + 1.5 × 8 = 3 + 12 = 15 m/s.

2. v_f = 0; use v_f² = v_i² + 2aΔx → 0 = 400 + 2(−4)Δx → Δx = 400/8 = 50 m.

09

Uniformly Accelerated Motion — Graphs

Theory

UAM produces different graph shapes compared to uniform motion:

Position-time graph (x vs. t): a parabola (curved line). The curve bends upward for positive acceleration, downward for negative acceleration.

Velocity-time graph (v vs. t): a straight diagonal line. The slope of this line equals the acceleration: slope = Δv / Δt = a. The area under the line equals Δx.

Acceleration-time graph (a vs. t): a horizontal line because acceleration is constant.

Worked Example

A v-t graph shows a straight line from (0 s, 0 m/s) to (6 s, 18 m/s). Find (a) the acceleration and (b) the displacement.

(a) slope: Δv / Δt = 18 / 6 = 3 m/s²
(b) area (triangle): ½ · base · height = ½ · 6 · 18 = 54 m

→ a = 3 m/s²  |  Δx = 54 m

Practice

  1. A v-t graph shows a line from (0 s, 10 m/s) to (4 s, 2 m/s). Find the acceleration. Is the object speeding up or slowing down?
  2. Sketch the shape you would expect for the x-t graph of an object that starts from rest and accelerates uniformly. Is it a straight line or a curve? Which way does it bend?
SHOW ANSWERS

1. Δv = 2 − 10 = −8 m/s; a = −8/4 = −2 m/s². Negative → slowing down.

2. It is a parabola curving upward (opening upward), because position increases faster and faster as the object gains speed.
